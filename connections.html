Connections: A Starter Kit


Primer Definition.

Users who wish to access the internet, are required to be connected in some way with their Internet Service Provider

Being connected wired or wirelessly allows data (packets) to be transmitted between your device and the internet servers



Did you know?

  • Fibre optics is the fastest wired broadband connection, with speeds up to 10 Gigabits per second (1 Gigabyte/sec);

  • 5G network is the latest mobile network generation and has promising speeds of up to 1 Gigabit per second, which is impressive in wireless terms;

  • Such speeds allow new possibilities in the digital world which may solve existing problems in regards to latency such as selfdriving cars, remote concerts and conferencing;

Types of Internet Connections

Fibre Optics

Fibre optic cable use lazer lights hich is known for being the fastest to transmit data. Each pulse represents in binary once it has been converted to digital signals

Copper ADSL

Copper wires are the most common connections, known as a ADSL line using existing telephone connections where each wire is in a twisted pair. Its outdated nature means its speed is annihilated by its future counterparts which use light instead of electricity

Wireless

Wireless mediums are possible especially in remote areas where cables cannot run for greater distances.
Using a satellite or transmission tower and a receiver, electromagnetic waves are sent among them. The receiver converts it into electrical signals for it to be useable by a router.
